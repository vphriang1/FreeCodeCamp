Introduction

Bokator is an ancient Khmer martial art. It was a form of self defense utilized by the Khmer empire. This empire reigned during 1000 A.D. The art encompasses weapon to weapon combat, hand to hand combat, and strategic battle formations. The art itself reflects the philosophy and lifestyle of the ancient Khmer.

Akma is a non-profit organization aimed at preserving Bokator for the next generation. Having a sense of regret that the world may lose Bokator entirely, Master Chet S. embarked on a journey to Cambodia to learn Bokator. In his journey he met various elders who knew the martial art. After years of intense scholarly research, Master Chet S. currently devotes his time to sharing Bokator with the community of Long Beach California with hopes to preserve this rarely practiced martial art.

Bokator

Bokator was concocted by the ancient Brahms or high priests. It follows the Vedic philosophy and ideals. In Vedic philosophy, there are many systems of self-study meant to improve a person's well being. Along with poetry, anatomy, natural sciences, and cosmological studies, Bokator was aimed at preserving and creating a better society. Appropriately, a Bokator practictioner's duty was to uphold honor for the mind, body, and nature. The Brahms tested Bokator on the battlefield and introduced it to other allies of the Khmer empire.

This martial art encapsulates hand to hand combat, weapons combat, military strategy, spiritual philosophy, and various forms of animal influenced fighting postures. For hand to hand combat Bokator significantly uses the hands, elbows, legs, and knees. The arm and leg strikes are uniquely named after personalities within the Ramayana epic. And the military stategies are based on the concept Bokator calls 'doors.' Thus with all of these components combined Bokator proves to be a flexible fluid martial art featuring many diverse applications.

Bokator techniques are named after many signatured warriors of the Ramayana epic.

Prerequisites

Acceptance into the program are very basic. They include:

  • The ability to recognize one's right from left
  • Is independently willing to learn
  • Willing to adhere to instruction from the master
  • Commitment to attend class regularly
  • Willing to bend one's knees to very low standards
  • Demonstrate proper ettiquette to the master and fellow students
  • Must be a person of good moral character

In this class every student must follow the basic customs of Khmer culture. This includes respecting the Lok Kru (meaning teacher) by offering him a traditional Asiatic bow, using appropriate language at all times, applying the etiqutte for martial arts, and by concluding each session with a brief peaceful ritual.
Concluding ritual

The ritual is conducted by the senior student leading the class. After each training session, everybody will follow the class leader by bowing to the earth with knuckles touched stating "respecting the earth." Then the ritual proceeds by placing both fists near the heart and stating 'honesty.' Then the ritual proceeds by bringing both fists to the forehead and stating 'wisdom.' These three statements as one revolution is repeated two more times. Finally, the class leader will invoke a small period of silent meditation.

Scarf designation

The practioner's level of expertise is classified into different colored scarfs. It's ascending order are:

  1. Purple scarf
  2. Green scarf
  3. Orange scarf
  4. Blue scarf
  5. Pink scarf
  6. Brown scarf
  7. Red scarf
The purple signifies a student in the beginner's level. A brown scarf signifies a fully competent student in the Bokator.

The red scarf is a unique scarf which signifies someone who has proved his mastery in Bokator. It is much more difficult to achieve this scarf in comparison to the rest of the scarfs. In most cases students who complete the program work their way up to obtain a brown scarf. A brown scarf in the Bokator paragdim symbolizes a black belt's degree.
May or essential components

In Khmer, may means leader in certain contexts. It could also signify the main component of a unit. For instance, 'may-sow' or a master key is a unique key amongst other keys. 'May' in the Bokator application consists of named self defense techniques that act as components or sub-components to the integrity of the art. Every unit of Bokator comprises of 'may' concepts. By learning the 'may's of Bokator, a practioner is able to conceptualize his offensive and defensive opportunities. Many unique 'may's mirror each other which allows for a pair-wise study. Aggregrating the 'may's constitute the science of ancient Khmer self defense.

The 'may's are the essenital components of Bokator. They are flexible fluid self defense techniques.
Straight line training

This training involves proceeding forwards and backwards on a conceptual straight line.

Motion exercise

Known as 'catching air,' the student extends his arm with palms facing the sky, pulls his hand back, forms a fist, and extends his arm outwards as if he were punching. The full release should have involved a twist to mimic a successful punch.

Authenticity

Authentic Bokator trains heavily in lower animal forms which require the bending of the knees for more fluid Bokator movement.
